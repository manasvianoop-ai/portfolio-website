Soft Horizons

2026 Conestoga College · Interior Decorating

Soft Horizons is a full residential Capstone project designed for Jada and Jordan, a newly married Millennial couple living in a 19th-floor waterfront condo in Bronte Village, Oakville. Nestled next to Lake Ontario, the home was conceived as a calm, light-filled space that supports clarity, comfort, and everyday ease. Inspired by their love of travel, outdoor living, and hosting, the design blends warmth, functionality, and elegance through subtle contrasts, layered lighting, and warm materials and textures that feel inviting and effortless to move through.

Sustainability and craftsmanship were central client values throughout. Material selections across the entryway, open-plan living and dining area, kitchen, and bedrooms prioritise natural, quality-driven options — warm wood finishes, stone surfaces, and organic textiles that are both environmentally thoughtful and built to last. The layout draws in natural light generously across every space, taking full advantage of the waterfront setting and reducing reliance on artificial sources throughout the day.

Universal Design was a core requirement of the brief. With Jada's father Dan (70) visiting frequently, the home needed to be fully accessible without sacrificing its aesthetic quality. The primary bedroom and bathroom were designed with complete UD compliance: barrier-free layouts, clear floor clearances, lever-style hardware, and adaptable fixtures that support independence at every stage of life. Soft Horizons is proof that inclusive design and refined style are not in conflict — when approached with intention, they strengthen each other.
